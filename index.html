The market

You did everything right.
And the market still went quiet.

Why the DACH region doesn't behave like any market you've entered before — and what actually moves it.

Your product works. Clients in London, Dubai, New York or Singapore pay for it, renew it, refer it. So you turned to the largest economy in Europe — a market that, on paper, needs exactly what you've built.

You hired an SDR. You took the conference booth. You sent the sequences. And then: a polite silence. The Landesbank that downloaded your whitepaper never replied. The asset manager who seemed genuinely interested "took it to the committee" — and the committee took it nowhere.

Six months in, your CRM is full of German names and empty of German revenue.

This market was never going to answer outreach. The decision isn't made in your demo — it's made in the conversation about it, after you've left the room.

German, Austrian and Swiss institutions buy slowly, by committee, and almost exclusively from people they already trust. Access here cannot be bought with budget or bought back with patience. It has to be borrowed — from someone who already holds it.

Three things outsiders underestimate

It isn't that they don't want your product. It's that they can't reach a yes the way you expect.

01

The committee, not the champion

Your enthusiastic contact rarely has the authority to buy. The real decision happens in a risk, compliance and procurement gauntlet you never see — and one unanswered concern quietly kills the deal.

02

The language behind the language

It isn't only German. It's knowing which words reassure a Vorstand, how risk appetite is really framed, and what "we'll get back to you" actually means. Nuance read wrong reads as risk.

03

Trust transfers; it doesn't scale

You cannot brute-force your way into trusted-vendor status. But it can be lent to you — when someone the institution already trusts puts their own name behind the introduction.

About

A network built to solve one problem: access.

DACH·GTM exists because the hardest part of selling into German-speaking finance was never the product — it was getting into the room.

What DACH·GTM is

One brand. A bench of senior operators.

DACH·GTM is a go-to-market network built specifically for crypto, digital-asset, Web3 and DeFi firms entering Germany, Austria and Switzerland. Behind one brand sits a small, curated bench of senior operators — each with 10+ years inside the region's banks, exchanges and asset managers, at least three years in digital assets, and their own live relationships.

We handle the brand, the marketing and the client relationship; the matched operator brings the network that opens the doors. For you, that means one trusted point of contact — and behind it, the right person for your segment, rather than whoever happens to be free.

Founder

DACH·GTM was founded by Katharina Schreiner, who has spent over a decade building and closing relationships across European and Asian financial markets — at Deutsche Bank, Commerzbank, Börse Stuttgart Group and the London Stock Exchange Group.

Her work experience spans the full width of the capital-markets stack: equities trading and research, post-trade, correspondent banking, payments, trade finance, debt, market data and analytics, digital-assets and crypto. That path built a network reaching across the institutions that move DACH. It also set the standard the network now hires to: every operator brings 10+ years in DACH financial-markets sales and at least three years inside crypto, digital assets, Web3 and DeFi.

FAQ

The questions serious buyers ask first.

Straight answers on how this works, what it costs you in risk, and where the limits are.

Who actually does the work?
A senior operator from the DACH·GTM network, matched to your segment — someone who already holds the relevant relationships with the banks, exchanges or asset managers you need to reach. You get one consistent point of contact, backed by the brand.
Who are the operators?
Senior go-to-market professionals, each with at least 10 years in DACH financial-markets sales and 3+ years in crypto, digital assets, Web3 and DeFi — and their own live network. We never field juniors or generalists, and we vet every operator to the founder's standard.
Are we hiring employees or contractors?
Contractors. You get senior representation under your own brand — even with an email address and business card in your company's name — without the cost, risk and management overhead of a local hire. The engagement is a service contract, not employment.
How is this priced?
It depends on the engagement. The Market Scan is a fixed scope for a fixed fee. The Fractional GTM Lead mandate is a monthly retainer plus a success component tied to closed business, so incentives align. The Door Opener is success-only. We agree the commercials in our first call.
Will you work with our competitors?
Never two directly competing firms at the same time. Exclusivity is by segment — that protects the value of every introduction made on your behalf.
How quickly will we see meetings?
After a Market Scan (4–6 weeks) we have a prioritised target list; warm introductions typically begin from month two of a mandate. This market rewards patience over volume — but the meetings that happen are real, not gatekeeper calls.
What if DACH isn't the right move for us yet?
We'll tell you — ideally during the Scan, before you commit to a long mandate. A clear "not yet, and here's why" is worth more than a year of polite silence.
We're not based in the EU — can you still help?
Yes. Most of our prospective clients sit outside DACH — in the UK, the Emirates, the US or Asia. That's the point: we're the local presence you don't have.
Do you guarantee deals?
No serious partner guarantees institutional deals. What we offer is genuine access and aligned incentives: with a success component, we're paid most when you win.
How do we start?
A 30-minute intro call. You tell us your product and target; we tell you honestly whether and how we can open doors. If it's a fit, we usually begin with a Market Scan.

Still have a question?

Ask it directly. A short call answers more than any FAQ can.
